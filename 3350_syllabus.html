COMP 3350 - Software Engineering I

Course Description

Introduction to software engineering. Software life cycle models, system and software requirements analysis, specifications, software design, testing and maintenance, software quality. Prerequisites: 6 credit hours of COMP courses at the 3000 level or ECE 3740.

Course goals

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

  • Compare agile and rigorous software development.
  • Integrate testing with implementation to ensure thorough test coverage.
  • Develop software to evolving requirements.
  • Refine software as it is being developed.
  • Maintain an existing software system.
  • Develop code in a team and share code safely amongst the members.
  • Use an agile approach to facilitate incremental development.
  • As part of a team, design and build a complete software product to solve a real-world problem.

Course Deliverables

Project

The project is a group project that will be submitted in several stages (called “iterations”) and will include a final project presentation. The mark value of each iteration of the project will be given as part of the description of the requirements for that iteration.

Due dates for each of the project iterations are found on the schedule. Late submissions will not receive any marks.

Each student on a project team will be quantitatively evaluated by the other members of the team on the student’s participation. This evaluation will be used to scale the team’s project mark for the individual student. Details will be presented in class.

Assignment

There will be one assignment separate from the project that will be done independently. See schedule for due date. The assignment must be submitted to pass the course.

Term Tests/Quizzes

There will be weekly quizzes throughout the term. Quizzes will be held in person during class time. The quizzes will be completed on UMLearn. You must be present in the room, you must bring a laptop or other device with you to write the quiz. No exceptions will be made to this. If you miss a quiz, there are no make-ups. If you are late, you will be allowed to write, but the cut-off time will still be at the same time as the rest of the class. Each week's first 30 minutes of class time on the first day of class will be dedicated to the quiz. You will be given 30 minutes, and the quizzes must be done without the help of chatGPT or other genAI tools. You can make handwritten (on paper or notability/good notes style app) notes and reference them during the quizzes. You must have these printed off. You must have written them yourself, and you are not allowed to use anything outside those notes. Quizzes will be done in project groups; however, everyone must submit their own quiz. There is a Quiz0 in week 0 that you can try out to ensure you understand how to use UMLearn's quiz features. 

There will be a final, written, in-class test towards the end of the term. Tentative dates for the test/quizzes are posted on the course timetable. You must submit a temporary leave of absence form if you miss the in-class end-of-term test. You will be offered two potential make-up dates in weeks 12/13. Failure to write during your makeup date will result in a 0 on the final term test.

Participation

In-class participation is required in this course. In class, your team will work on the project or concepts that feed into the project. Attendance will be taken every class. You may have up to 3 (A01) or 2 (A02) unexcused absences with no penalty to your grade. These are not freebies. You must submit a temporary leave of absence form to your instructor as per the universities policies.  Every class missed after that means a 2% deduction to your final grade in the course. You must attend at least 80% of the scheduled classes to pass the course.

Academic Integrity

Academic misconduct is a very serious offense and will be dealt with in accordance with the University’s discipline bylaw. Examples of academic misconduct include:

  • submitting work which is not entirely your own, or providing your work to another student or students;
  • use of unauthorized material or inappropriate collaboration while producing work;
  • writing any part of work for another person; or
  • having another person write any part of your work for you,

where “work” includes any material submitted for evaluation in the course. This includes assignments, labs, tests, and final examinations.

You will be required to confirm your agreement to an Honesty Declaration before submitting assignments or writing any test or exam.

All students in this course must read and meet the expectations described in the Expectations for Individual Work in Computer Science. Failure to do so can result in serious consequences.

Specifically, in COMP 3350:

  • The project that you will build with your team is group work and, as such, is not subject to the same individual coursework; you are explicitly permitted to work on a team to complete the project. All code and deliverables that you and your team submit as part of the project must be written by you and your team, or otherwise adequately cited.
  • The individual assignment and tests in the course are individual coursework and must be completed independently.

More detailed instructions and expectations may be provided with each activity.

If you are ever uncertain about what constitutes academic misconduct, please contact your instructor before you submit questionable material for grading.

Illness/Temporary Leave of Absence

Due to the group-based nature of this course, most deadlines are not flexible, as very few deliverables are completed individually. For group projects, if you are sick at any point, deadlines will remain unchanged. It is your responsibility to communicate with your group members to determine how to handle the situation. Just as in a real-world scenario, if one team member is unavailable, the rest of the team steps in to ensure progress. Group member evaluations are part of the course and directly impact grades. Therefore, if you are frequently unavailable throughout the course, it will affect your final grade.

The major assignment will be released toward the end of the term, and you will have approximately two weeks to complete it. If you cannot meet the deadline, you must submit a Temporary Leave of Absence form at least 24 hours before the due date to request a 2-day extension. This form must be submitted to your instructor for approval.

Quizzes are short and weekly. Please note that missing a quiz will result in a grade of 0, as there are no make-up quizzes or extensions available.

The final term test is tentatively scheduled during class time, as noted in the course schedule. If you cannot write the test due to illness, you must submit a Temporary Leave of Absence form between 12 and 24 hours before the test to inform your instructor. If you recover in time and space is available in another section, you may be allowed to write with that section. Otherwise, you can write the test during week 12 or 13 (dates TBD). Failure to write the final term test by the end of the term will result in a grade of 0.

You may only submit one Temporary Leave of Absence form per term in this course.  If you require additional accommodations, you will be referred to this policy and directed to contact a faculty advisor.
